Our story

Made slowly,
eaten fast.

We're a family-run scoop shop obsessed with real ingredients, bold flavors, and the kind of summer afternoon that smells like waffle cones.

🍦
🍨

A backyard experiment that got out of hand

It started in 2009 with a secondhand churn, a bag of Mindanao cacao, and a bet between two sisters that they could make better chocolate ice cream than anything in town.

They won the bet. Then they won a farmers' market stall. Then a shop on Elm Street, which ran out of parking on opening day.

Fifteen years later, we're still making every batch by hand — the same way, the same people, the same obsession with getting it right before we serve it to you.

What we believe about ice cream

🌿

Real ingredients only

No artificial anything. If we can't source it from a farm we've visited, it doesn't go in the batch.

🕐

Slow churned

We churn at low speed to keep less air in the mix. Denser, richer, and it holds up better in the sun.

🔁

Seasonal rotation

Our menu changes with what's good right now. You'll find mango in July and blood orange in February.
